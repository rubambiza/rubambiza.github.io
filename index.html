Gloire Rubambiza | [CV]

I am a second year CS Ph.D. student at Cornell University, advised by Hakim Weatherspoon. Before joining Cornell, I received a B.S. in Computer Science at Grand Valley State University (GVSU).

Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of Systems & Networking and Information and Communication Technologies for Development (ICTD). Broadly, I am interested in developing technologies for populations traditionally under-served by Computer Science research.
Currently, I am working on two projects: (1) the first distributed, partition-tolerant log fit for IoT deployment (Vegvisir), and (2) leveraging Microsoft's FarmBeats IoT platform to make IoT sensing and data analytics accessible and financially profitable for smallholder farmers in the U.S.
In the past, with funding from the McNair Scholars and Leadership Alliance programs, I conducted research ranging from new algorithms to improve African Internet traffic routing to detecting the failure of submarine Internet cables using available bandwidth and packet routing.
